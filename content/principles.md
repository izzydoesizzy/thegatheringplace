# The Gathering Place — Culture Principles

*These aren't rules. They're the culture. The difference matters.*

---

## The Core Principles

**No performance required.**
You don't have to be "on." Nobody here needs you to be impressive. Leave the stage persona at the door. That's the whole point.

**Pause before answering.**
A beat of reflection before speaking. It sounds small. It changes everything. You'll find yourself saying things you actually mean instead of things you've already rehearsed.

**Ask more than you answer.**
The most interesting people in any room are the ones who want to know more than they want to be known.

**Don't speak for more than 90 seconds straight before 5pm.**
Not a rule. A cultural nudge. Talky, smart people can fill every room. This slows the room down enough for something real to happen.

---

## The Signals

The Gathering Place uses a few simple, opt-in signals to communicate presence without social obligation.

**Outside your door / workspace:**
- *Open* — drop-ins welcome, glad to have you
- *Working* — focused, but a brief hello is fine
- *Deep focus* — please come back later

There's no social contract here. Nobody owes anyone their attention.

---

## The Am I Crazy Room

Every location has a designated space — a chair, a corner, a spot by the fire. If someone is in it, it means one thing: *I have an idea I want someone to hear.*

It's an opt-in signal. You don't get to spew your ideas at people. But if you're in the chair, anyone can come sit across from you. And if you sit across from someone in the chair, you're agreeing to listen first and react second.

Nobody wanders in by accident. Nobody gets ambushed.

---

## The Fire

By the fire, none of the above applies.

You can just be.

---

## What This Isn't

This is not a mastermind. There is no curriculum, no weekly accountability thread, no scorecards. Nobody is going to hold your feet to the fire about the thing you said you'd do.

What it is: a place where you're around people operating at a level where *you* don't want to waste their time. That's more motivating than any accountability structure.

## West Wing Wednesday (Pilot Week Tradition)

During the pilot week (Feb 2026): West Wing episodes from noon to midnight. No agenda. If you want to watch, you watch. If you don't, you don't. Some of the best conversations start when the show goes quiet.
