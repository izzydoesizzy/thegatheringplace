# The Gathering Place

> A private membership club for creative professionals, founded by Drew Dudley.

**Live site:** https://izzydoesizzy.github.io/thegatheringplace

---

## What This Is

This repository contains everything related to **The Gathering Place** — the brand, the vision, the documents, and the public-facing website.

The Gathering Place is a physical, membership-based creative club. A rotating series of high-end properties designed for speakers, coaches, writers, designers, and other creative professionals who need a place to think, work, create, and be around people who "get it" without needing an explanation.

It is not a conference. It is not a co-working space. It is not an event series. The closest analogy Drew offered was "a year-long conference drop-in, entirely run by your energy."

---

## The Founder

**Drew Dudley** — professional speaker, Day One Leadership, 1,000+ keynotes over 20 years.

After twenty years on the road, Drew noticed something: his brain had stopped saying *oh, that's interesting* about being on stage. So he started designing the thing he actually wanted — not a program, not a curriculum, not a conference. Every time he imagined it, the same image kept coming back: gathered around a fire with people he found genuinely interesting. No agenda. No performance pressure.

The Gathering Place is that image turned into a business.

## Membership

- **12 founding seats** — hand-picked, not open enrollment
- **$2,500/month** (~$30,000/year) — terms are flexible, price is not
- **6 members** = financially sustainable (break-even)
- **Fully negotiable terms** — but the seat is worth more than revenue in year one

What's included:
- Physical access to all rotating properties
- Professional on-site video studio (keynotes, social content)
- Social media production through studio partnership
- Host your own retreats (bring 3 guests, bill them directly)
- Drew's presence — coaching, fireside conversations, idea-bouncing

## The Culture

> *"Can I create a place where people feel safer and freer to be their sort of weirder creative selves?"*

The Gathering Place has four core principles — not rules, a culture:

1. **No performance required.** Leave the stage persona at the door.
2. **Pause before answering.** A beat of reflection before speaking changes everything.
3. **Ask more than you answer.** The most interesting people want to know more than they want to be known.
4. **Don't speak for more than 90 seconds straight before 5pm.** A nudge, not a mandate.

By the fire, none of the above applies.

### The Am I Crazy Room
Every location has a designated chair or space. If you're sitting in it, it means one thing: *I have an idea I want someone to hear.* Nobody gets ambushed. The chair is the signal.

## The Green Room

Inside The Gathering Place is a sub-community called **The Green Room** — designed specifically for professional speakers. Not exclusively for them (the room works better when it's not all one thing), but as the beating heart of the club.

The Green Room won't be advertised. It won't have a page. It will spread the way all good rooms spread — by word of mouth from the people inside it.

*Not a place you sign up for. A place you're asked into.*

## Brand Direction

Drew's instinctive visual image:

- **Medieval / wrought iron** — knights around a round table, fire at the center
- **Central motif: fire** — the oldest place where stories and wisdom were passed around
- **An emblem, not a logo** — something you could put on a ring or see wrought in an iron gate
- **Not hoity-toity** — earned, not bought. Classy and subtle.
- **The Green Room sub-brand** — same family as the parent mark, slight variation (different color, extra element — the way a military unit wears a distinguishing badge)
